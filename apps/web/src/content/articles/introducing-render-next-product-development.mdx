Render Next is being repositioned around practical product development: custom web apps, SaaS MVPs, dashboards, product websites, and interactive product presentation.

The goal is not to be a generic web agency or a personal developer portfolio. The goal is to build software that supports real business workflows.

That usually means a project needs more than a nice landing page. It needs clear product structure, working interfaces, data models, user flows, and implementation decisions that make sense after launch.

## What Render Next builds

Render Next focuses on four connected types of work:

- custom web applications and dashboards
- SaaS MVPs and product platforms
- product websites and launch pages
- interactive 3D product presentation for configurable products

This mix is intentional. The main work is product development. 3D rendering and interactive product visualization are included when they help explain, configure, or sell a product online.

## Why workflows matter

Many businesses do not need a generic website. They need a better way to handle a specific process.

That process might be creating proposals, presenting product options, managing a catalog, showing materials and variants, or helping buyers understand a configurable product before they contact sales.

When the workflow is the product, the website and the software need to be designed together.

## Current product work

Two public products show the direction:

- FurnishAR — an interactive 3D and AR product presentation platform for furniture brands.
- Propunem — a proposal and client presentation platform for service businesses.

They are different products, but they share the same principle: software should make a business workflow clearer, easier to use, and easier to present.
